Methodological Considerations

Case-Crossover Design

Case-crossover studies usually require (quoted from Columbia Mailman SPH):

  • Short-term changes in exposure
  • Transient changes in disease risk
  • Acute-onset disease

My main questions with regards to our data:

  • What is the time threshold at which disease risk becomes transient? Can we consider wasting, with a lag period of weeks to potentially months, transient?
  • Similarly, there is uncertainty surrounding the actual onset of wasting. Would it make sense to investigate these extreme events as triggers that tip a child to acute malnutrition?
  • Finally, in a case-crossover analysis of these data, would these potential lag structures interfere with our ability to select our case and control windows?

Log-Binomal Analysis of Cross-Sectional Data

Similar to our previous analyses, these would entail comparison of wasting prevalences across differing levels of exposure, adjusting for confounders. This would produce adjusted Prevalence Ratios.

Questions here:

  • How do we investigate lags here - should it be a formal hypothesis testing process, or included in our exploratory analyses?
  • Weve been looking at extreme precipitation and temperature events, but less so at anomalously low values. Do we want to include these as well (e.g.drought)?

Exploratory Analyses

I tried exploring the potential lag structures of temperature and heat anomalies.

Adjusted log-binomial models were run as follows (basic example code):

logbin(
  data = data,
  formula = y ~ x +    ## substitute vector of lags for x
    surv_area + moy + year
)
